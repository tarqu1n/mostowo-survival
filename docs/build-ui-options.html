You should see what you're building —
and place a whole run in one drag.

Today, placement is a green square: no picture of the structure, no facing, no build animation, no honest progress, and one tap per tile. Three directions for fixing it — same problems, three philosophies about where the feedback lives. All three are drawn mobile-first.

Mobile is the primary platform — thumb-zone controls, drag-to-paint a line of walls or traps
No image No rotation No animation No progress One tap per tile
OPTION A

Faithful Ghost

world-first · lowest cost

Almost no new HUD chrome — the canvas does the talking. The ghost becomes a real textured sprite that flips as you rotate; press-drag along the ground to paint a run of walls/traps and release to queue them all; a progress bar floats over each site while a worker builds it. Reuses the wall's per-orientation art and the harvest progress bar that already ship.

OPTION B

Build Card

HUD-first · thumb-native

A bottom sheet is the source of truth, sized for one-handed play. Icon, cost, a four-way facing row, and — as you drag a run on the world — a live queue total (count + total cost) with one big Confirm. Under-construction progress lives in the sheet, so you never squint at the canvas.

OPTION C

Blueprint Mode

immersive · highest ceiling

Building becomes its own moment. The world dims, a snap grid lights up, a rotation ring wraps the cursor, and dragging lays a snapped run of ghosts — the commit bar tallies count, total cost and worker ETA, then one Confirm queues the lot. Construction animates scaffold → build → settle. The Factorio/Valheim treatment.

My lean: ship A, grow into B

A fixes the two things you feel most — no image, no rotation — plus drag-to-paint the runs of walls and traps you actually build every night, at the lowest cost, reusing the wall's orientation art and the harvest progress bar that already exist. B's sheet then layers on with no rework and is the most thumb-native. C is the destination if building becomes a headline pillar, but it's a data-model + input + art commitment.
